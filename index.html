about

my name is erik halliwell. i currently reside in edmonton, on earth. i'm completing my master's at ualberta in electrical engineering.

in grad school, i've been spending time

  • on my master's research, which explores the use of optical methods like satellite laser ranging and laser communication systems to do spacecraft orbit determination - trying to out-do radio-based systems by 5 order of magnitude
  • with PEPPER-X - a team I led which flight-qualified two energetic particle precipitation scientific instruments on-board NASA's RockSat-X sounding rocket. they'll now fly on the $20MM RADICALS microsatellite in 2027
  • as the lead teaching assistant for the 4th-year computer engineering capstone course (and won two awards doing it)
  • building and flying a LoRa-based communications system for a stratospheric weather balloon which i tracked with APRS (callsign VA6CSC, mostly on 2m but sometimes 70cm)
  • hucking backflips on skis with my friends and built an instagram page with 65k followers in the process

before that, i was doing my undergrad at ualberta in engineering physics, and

  • created open-source deployable solar panels (flown on 3 satellites and soon a 4th)
  • was a primary operator for alberta's first satellite, Ex-Alta 1, built by AlbertaSat
  • got involved with free-space optical communication systems by building one that implemented LDPC FEC to achieve BER <10e-6 in LEO-to-Earth levels of emulated turbulence
  • worked for two summers at G2V Optics building LED-based solar simulator calibration tools
  • designed my own guitar pedals with the Pod/ICE Incubator, winning best hardware hack at the 2021 ualberta hackathon
  • drove 35 hrs to/from eastern canada to test a multispectral imager that me and my friends built on-board a canadian/french space agency balloon
  • rocked out as the guitarist/band coodinator for the Engineering Physics Club
  • got paid to run north america's largest competitive dodgeball league, the ualberta-residence-based Lister Dodgeball League
  • consumed ~25kg of coffee (yes, i tracked it)

on holidays, i'll be out spending time in the canadian rockies, skiing (backcountry and resort), leading my slo-pitch team to their 4th ualberta intramurals title in a row, or browsing wikipedia with the goal of getting on jeopardy one day.
